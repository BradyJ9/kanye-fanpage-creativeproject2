The College Dropout

Kanye's Freshman Debut

"[The College Dropout] features string arrangements, choirs and his signature soul sampling, frequently branded as chipmunk-esque for its sped-up and high pitched nature. Contrary to the popular gangster-persona lyrics that were so popular in hip-hop at the time, his lyrics mostly revolved around themes of family, materialism, religion and racism." -Genius

Highlights

  • All Falls Down

    Kanye provides a commentary and personally introspects on the effects of consumerism in this challenging song. Ye explores the perspective of a self-conscious young adult lost in a commercially driven lifestyle before admitting to his own insecurities that fuel his need to spend money to feel good about himself. The observable consumerism in hip hop culture is also called out, making this track on Ye's freshman album a bold social meta-commentary that raised eyebrows of many in the industry.

    "It seem we livin' the American Dream,
    But the people highest up got the lowest self-esteem,
    The prettiest people do the ugliest things,
    For the road to riches and diamond rings"

  • Jesus Walks

    The fourth single from the album was another powerful statement: a mainstream popular hip hop song centered around Christianity. Pondering on the evils of the world, Ye unapologetically proclaims his need and worship of Christ, fully aware of the stigma and dismissal with which religious motifs are often treated by pop culture.

    "So here go my single, dawg, radio needs this,
    They say you can rap about anything except for Jesus,
    That means guns, sex, lies, videotape,
    But if I talk about God my record won't get played, huh?"

  • Slow Jamz

    Kanye's first commercial hit and #1 single, "Slow Jamz" is exactly that: a smooth jazz infused soulful groove displaying Kanye's versatility in production. Guest feature Jamie Foxx provides the sweet vocals on the chorus, while Twista takes the last verse to switch up the pace with his characteristic speedy rap delivery. Kanye also showed his comedic side while providng an all-time memorable line referencing another famous popstar.

    "She got a light-skinned friend look like Michael Jackson,
    Got a dark-skinned friend look like Michael Jackson"

  • Through The Wire

    In a career defining song that characterizes Kanye's unrelenting grit and determination to make it in the rap game, Ye tells the story of his near-fatal car crash that had just recently happened to him at the time. He explains the reconstructive jaw surgery he underwent, and how even a scare with death could not stop him, clearly demonstrated as he was now -- even despite the metal headgear he temporarily was wearing post-surgery -- literally rapping "through the wire".

    "But I'm a champion, so I turned tragedy to triumph,
    Make music that's fire, spit my soul through the wire"

  • Last Call

    The outro to The College Dropout, "Last Call" shows Kanye reminiscing on his life up to this point and telling the story of his upcoming in the rap industry. The latter half of the extended song turns from rapping to narration as a humble and eager Kanye gives details on his grind. This song is a fan favorite to many, as it the struggles a young Kanye had to go through to make it in the game.

    "Now I could let these dream killers kill my self-esteem,
    Or use my arrogance as the steam to power my dreams,
    I use it as my gas, so they say that I'm gassed,
    But without it I'd be last, so I ought to laugh"
